Reading is one of the most fundamental skills a human being can develop. It opens doors to knowledge, culture, and imagination that would otherwise remain closed. When we read, we engage not just our eyes but our minds, making connections between ideas, questioning assumptions, and building new understanding.

There are many types of reading. Skimming is when a reader quickly looks over text to get a general idea of the content without reading every word. Scanning is used to find specific information quickly, such as looking for a name or a date. Deep reading, on the other hand, involves slow, thoughtful engagement with a text, where the reader reflects on meaning and builds comprehension layer by layer.

Reading comprehension depends on several factors including vocabulary, background knowledge, and attention. A reader with a rich vocabulary can understand more complex texts. Background knowledge helps readers connect new information to what they already know, making it easier to retain and understand.

The benefits of regular reading are well documented. Studies show that people who read regularly have better memory, stronger analytical skills, and higher empathy. Reading fiction in particular has been shown to improve a person's ability to understand other people's perspectives and emotions.

Digital reading has changed how people consume text. E-books, articles, and social media posts are now a major part of daily reading. However, research suggests that reading on screens leads to shallower processing compared to reading on paper, making deep comprehension harder to achieve.
